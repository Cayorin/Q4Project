The Weeknd

Abel Tesfaye, also known as the Weeknd, was born on February 16, 1990, in Toronto Canada. His first album was House of Balloons, which got publicized during 2011. He also holds four grammy awards.

In 2015, the Weeknd enjoyed huge success with the Grammy-winning album Beauty Behind the Madness, which boasted the hit single “Can’t Feel My Face.” His follow-up album, Starboy (2016), also won a Grammy and featured collaborations with artists such as Daft Punk.

The Weeknd, a prominent alternative R&B musician in the music industry, was born Abel Tesfaye in Toronto, in Canada, on February 16, 1990. Despite being the son of Ethiopian immigrants, Weeknd has not spent much time with his father, who abandoned the family while he was still a kid. Traditional Ethiopian music and Michael Jackson, the "King of Pop," were among his early musical influences. Jackson's 1979 smash song "Don't Stop 'til You Get Enough" was particularly highlighted by the Weeknd in an interview with Vanity Fair, describing it as the song that helped his discover his voice and his reason for singing
